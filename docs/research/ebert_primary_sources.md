# Primary Source Collections: Friedrich Ebert (1871-1925)

## 1. FES / ARCHIV DER SOZIALEN DEMOKRATIE (AdsD)

- **URL:** https://www.fes.de/archiv-der-sozialen-demokratie
- **Location:** Godesberger Allee 149, 53175 Bonn, Germany
- **Access:** Open to researchers; "Visual Library" portal provides online finding aids + selected digitized materials.

### What it holds:
- Ebert's personal papers (**Nachlass Friedrich Ebert**) among ~1,500 personliche Nachlasse.
- Digital finding aid (*Findbuch*) with classification scheme and reference codes accessible through the FES Visual Library.
- Recently acquired: papers of **Paul Runge**, containing two previously unknown handwritten Ebert letters from 1915.
- The AdsD is the "collective memory" of German Social Democracy -- it also holds SPD party archives, trade union records (ADGB, DGB, IG Metall), ~920 personal paper collections, ~4 million photographs, and 67,000 posters.
- The **Archiv fur Sozialgeschichte (AfS)** (published annually since 1961 by FES) has published Ebert-related document discoveries and source editions across many volumes.
- **FES Digital Library:** Full-text PDFs of FES publications including historical monographs on Ebert. URL: http://library.fes.de/library/html/english/full-infodigbib.html

### Critical caveat:
A large portion of Ebert's original personal papers was **destroyed during WWII**. Another portion passed to collector Alexander Stein in 1933 and has been lost. What survives is fragmentary.

## 2. BUNDESARCHIV (German Federal Archives)

- **URL:** https://www.bundesarchiv.de

The Bundesarchiv's investigation confirmed that **Ebert's complete personal Nachlass does not survive**. The Bundesarchiv holds **little of his original personal papers**.

### What it does hold:
- **Government records** from Ebert's presidency: Reich President's Office files (*Buro des Reichsprasidenten*, record group R 601), cabinet minutes, and related administrative records from 1919-1925.
- The cross-institutional union catalog for locating Ebert materials anywhere: **Zentrale Datenbank Nachlasse (ZDN)** -- https://www.bundesarchiv.de/DE/Navigation/Finden/Nachlassdatenbank/nachlassdatenbank.html

## 3. PUBLISHED COLLECTIONS OF EBERT'S WRITINGS

### A. The Classic Edition (1926)
- *Friedrich Ebert: Schriften, Aufzeichnungen, Reden. Mit unveroffentlichten Erinnerungen aus dem Nachlass*
- Editor: Friedrich Ebert jun.; biographical portrait by Paul Kampffmeyer
- Carl Reissner Verlag, Dresden, 1926. 2 vols. (Vol. 1: 384 pp., Vol. 2: 357 pp.)
- **Significance:** The foundational edition, drawing on Nachlass materials before their wartime destruction.
- WorldCat: OCLC 479819 | Open Library: OL6702196M

### B. Modern Critical Edition (2017)
- *Friedrich Ebert -- Reden als Reichsprasident (1919-1925)*
- Editor: **Walter Muhlhausen**
- J.H.W. Dietz Verlag, Bonn, 2017. 422 pp. ISBN: 978-3-8012-4234-3
- **Significance:** The authoritative scholarly edition of all presidential speeches, with full apparatus. Foreword by the German Federal President. This is Band 1 of the *Edition Friedrich Ebert Reden* series.

### C. Private Correspondence (1992)
- *Friedrich Ebert und seine Familie: Private Briefe, 1909-1924*
- Editors: Walter Muhlhausen with Bernd Braun
- R. Oldenbourg Verlag, Munchen, 1992. 178 pp., 66 illustrations. ISBN: 348655946X
- Schriftenreihe der Stiftung Reichsprasident-Friedrich-Ebert-Gedenkstatte, Band 3.
- **Status:** Out of print; available in research libraries.

### D. Additional:
- *Kampfe und Ziele* (Carl Reissner, Dresden, [1927], 407 pp.) -- includes reminiscences from friends.
- *Die Lage der Arbeiter im Bremer Backergewerbe* (Bremen, 1892, 24 pp.) -- Ebert's early trade-union study.

## 4. CONTEMPORARY MEMOIRS

### Gustav Noske -- Von Kiel bis Kapp: zur Geschichte der Deutschen Revolution
- Verlag fur Politik und Wirtschaft, Berlin, 1920. 211 pp.
- First-hand account of the revolution and the Ebert government's decisions from the man who served as Ebert's Reichswehrminister and suppressed the Spartacist uprising.

### Philipp Scheidemann -- Memoiren eines Sozialdemokraten
- Carl Reissner Verlag, Dresden, 1928. 2 vols., ~850 pp.
- Contains **unusually candid criticism of Ebert**, most famously their explosive 9 November 1918 confrontation when Ebert, "flushed dark red with anger," berated Scheidemann for proclaiming the republic. Reveals Ebert's secret discussions about preserving the monarchy.
- English: *The Memoirs of Philipp Scheidemann* (trans. J.E. Michell, D. Appleton, 1929).
- Also: *Der Zusammenbruch* (1921) and an exile manuscript *Kritik der deutschen Sozialdemokratie* (c. 1935/36).

### Wilhelm Groener -- Lebenserinnerungen: Jugend, Generalstab, Weltkrieg
- Editor: Friedrich Freiherr Hiller von Gaertringen. Vandenhoeck & Ruprecht, Gottingen, 1957. 584 pp.
- Posthumous. Contains Groener's account of the **Ebert-Groener Pact** (November 1918).
- Translated excerpt: https://germanhistorydocs.org/en/weimar-germany-1918-1933/ghdi:document-4014

### Otto Gessler -- Reichswehrpolitik in der Weimarer Zeit
- Editor: Kurt Sendtner; foreword by Theodor Heuss. Deutsche Verlags-Anstalt, Stuttgart, 1958. 582 pp.
- Gessler was Reichswehrminister 1920-1928 (appointed by Ebert). Contains **paired character portraits of Ebert and Hindenburg**. Covers the Seeckt insubordination and the Lohmann Affair.

### Arnold Brecht -- Aus nachster Nahe: Lebenserinnerungen 1884-1927
- Deutsche Verlags-Anstalt, Stuttgart, 1966 (English: *The Political Education of Arnold Brecht*, Princeton University Press, 1970).
- Brecht was a senior civil servant in the Reich Chancellery during Ebert's presidency. Provides detailed first-hand observations of Ebert's working methods, political decision-making, and interactions with ministers from inside the government apparatus.
- Uniquely valuable for understanding Ebert's day-to-day governance and administrative style.

## 5. REICHSTAG PROTOCOLS

- **URL:** https://www.reichstagsprotokolle.de/
- **Access:** Free, open, full-text searchable (OCR for Fraktur).

Covers all Reichstag sessions 1867-1942, including the Weimar National Assembly (1919-1920). Searchable by speaker, date, and keyword.

### Ebert's 6 February 1919 opening speech:
*Verhandlungen der verfassunggebenden Deutschen Nationalversammlung*, Vol. 1, pp. 1-13 (1. Sitzung).

### English translations of key speeches online:
- 6 Feb 1919: https://germanhistorydocs.org/en/weimar-germany-1918-1933/ghdi:image-4158
- 11 Feb 1919: https://alphahistory.com/weimarrepublic/groener-ebert-pact-1957/ (via FirstWorldWar.com / Wayback Machine)

### Audio recording:
Michigan State University G. Robert Vincent Voice Library holds Ebert's 1919 address as a sound recording: https://d.lib.msu.edu

## 6. FRIEDRICH-EBERT-GEDENKSTATTE (Heidelberg)

- **URL:** https://www.ebert-gedenkstaette.de
- **Access:** Small museum/archive; in-person research by arrangement.

### Holdings:
- Museum exhibition (updated 2007)
- Library (~7,000 volumes)
- Archive (~60 Ebert autograph documents + personal belongings)
- No major digitization project identified -- distinct from the FES/AdsD in Bonn.
- Publishes the *Schriftenreihe* series that includes the Muhlhausen editions.

## 9. DIPLOMATIC CORRESPONDENCE

### Politisches Archiv des Auswartigen Amts (PA AA)
- **Location:** Berlin (in-person access)
- Holds Ebert-period diplomatic correspondence including instructions to ambassadors and foreign policy papers from the Reich President's office, 1919-1925.

### Published diplomatic documents:
- **Akten zur deutschen auswartigen Politik 1918-1945** (Vandenhoeck & Ruprecht, Gottingen). Multi-volume series publishing German foreign policy documents, with some volumes having English-language companion editions in the *Documents on German Foreign Policy 1918-1945* series. Ebert's role in foreign policy decisions and his communications with foreign heads of state appear throughout the 1918-1925 volumes.
- Available at major research libraries and partially via the Bundesarchiv's digital offerings.
